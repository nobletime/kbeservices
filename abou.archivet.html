About

NeuroRex Inc. is a small medical company in Houston developing new technologies for neurology and neuroscience that have the potential to advance the field, mainly improvements in sensor technologies for electroencephalogram (EEG) assessment and electrophysiology biomarkers.

 

Team

Robert K. Bradley PhD, Director of material Eng.
Dr. Bradley is the co-inventor of NeuroRex patended dry-electrodes. He received his PhD in physical chemistry at Rice University under Nobel Laureate Richard Smalley working on the development of the HiPCO reactor for scalable production of carbon nanotubes. He has worked for several startup companies and spent 5 years at NASA Johnson Space Center developing nanotechnology-driven applications supporting future missions. Dr. Bradley's previous experience in medical device development were at Nanospectra LLC and Remicalm LLC; in particular at Remicalm he played a key role in development and go-to-market of the Identafi 3000Â® oral cancer detection device. At NASA, he organized the Astronaut health management section of the National Nanotechnology Initiative Grand Challenge. Dr. Bradley has been part of engineering design teams at several startup companies as well as at NASA. He brings a broad functional knowledge base that spans device design, materials, programming, statistics and chemistry with a fair amount of knowledge with respect to business aspects of commercializing technology.

 

Alexander Rotenberg MD PhD, Medical Advisor
Dr. Rotenberg is a practicing neurologist and epileptologist at Boston Childeren Hospital. He is an active member of the American Epilepsy Society and is the recipient of the 2016 Dreifuss-Penry Epilepsy Award from the American Academy of Neurology. He is a graduate of Johns Hopkins University (BA) and the State University of New York, Downstate Medical Center (MD, PhD).
