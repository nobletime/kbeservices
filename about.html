Key Team

We are a team of scientists, engineers and entrepreneurs developing Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) compatible neural electrodes and acquisition system for electrophysiology recording and brain stimulation. Our initial research started out in Analog Brain Imaging Laboratory that paved the way for early stage prototypes to develop and lab bench tested, largely supported with funding from the National Institutes of Health.

Hernan Millan (Director of R&D): Mr. Millan is the lead engineer of High-field One system, a data acquisition system for recording of Electrophysiology signals during high-field MRI in human and primate. Mr. Millan brings 30+ years of experience leading projects, designing and engineering Integrated Circuits for various applications in the private sector. Mr. Millan has been part of the engineering and design team in small and mid-size companies where he played a key role in the company's prototype/product development cycle and provided important structure for developing and bringing new products to market. Separate from his experience in the private sector, Mr. Millan has worked with NIH-funded investigators at Massachusetts General Hospital since 2004, where he integrated tightly and demonstrated successful collaboration with the research teams. Mr. Millan received his Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering from Northeastern University in Boston, Massachusetts in 1978.

Dr. Giorgio Bonmassar: Dr. Bonmassar is the co-founder of EMRI Systems LLP., and an Associate Professor of Radiology at Harvard Medical School where he directs the Analog Brain Imaging Laboratory for electrophysiology and MRI at the Athinoula A. Martinos Center for Biomedical Imaging, Massachusetts General Hospital. Dr. Bonmassar is the inventor of patented MR compatible subdural electrodes (PTFOS) and High-field one system. Dr. Bonmassar's research has been funded by NIH, DoD and Whitaker Foundation grants. Dr. Bonmassar brings twenty years of research experience in bioelectromagnetic modeling, MRI Physics, and design and implementation of imaging compatible electrophysiological systems. Dr. Bonmassar received his Ph.D. from Boston University and completed his postdoctoral research in Radiology at Harvard Medical School in 2001.

Dr. Patrick Britz (Business Advisor): Dr. Britz is the former CEO of Brain Vision LLC and former V.P. of sales and marketing at Brain Products, one of the market-leading companies for EEG &fMRI research equipment. Dr. Britz has contributed to the economic success of research device companies such as CGX, NIRx, and CREMedical. Dr. Britz has been an invitee to the NIH workshop entitled "The Brain Initiative Transformative Technologies" to speak about new imaging tools that could make a long-lasting, transformative impact on human neuroscience in the near future.
